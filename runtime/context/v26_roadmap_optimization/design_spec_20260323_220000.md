# UX/UI Design Specification: Roadmap Optimization (Zoom & High-Density Planning)
**Project:** TaskFlow AI (Enterprise Task OS)
**Version:** 26.0 (Timeline Scaling & Navigation)
**Date:** 2026-03-23
**Designer:** Senior UX/UI Architect & Lead Designer

## 1. Problem Statement & Empathy Mapping
**User Pain Point:** *"feature roadmap calendar too long and hard for me"*
- **Current State:** The timeline is fixed at a daily resolution with a 40px cell width, leading to massive horizontal scrolling and a complete loss of "Big Picture" context.
- **Goal:** Redesign the Roadmap to support dynamic scaling (Zoom levels), improved temporal grouping, and faster navigation. The goal is to let the user see "The Quarter" as easily as "The Week".

## 2. Information Architecture (IA) Redesign

### 2.1 Multi-Scale Timeline (Zoom Levels)
Introduce a "View Options" toggle in the header:
- **Month View (Default for High-Level):** Cells represent Weeks. Best for 3-6 month planning.
- **Week View:** Cells represent Days. Best for 2-4 week execution.
- **Day View (Legacy):** High-density day resolution for specific task tracking.

### 2.2 Visual Hierarchy & Grouping
- **Month Header:** A secondary sticky header row that groups days/weeks under their respective Month (e.g., "March 2026").
- **Smart Bounds:** The timeline will center on "Today" by default and provide a "Jump to Today" quick action.

### 2.3 Visual Refinements (Merkle CI)
- **Compact Rows:** Reduce row height to `h-8` or `h-9` for higher data density.
- **Task Bar Labels:** Move task titles *inside* the bars if long enough, or immediately to the *right* of the bar if the bar is too short. This prevents the "Left Pane" from being the only source of truth.

## 3. UI Flow & State Management
- **Local State (`viewMode`):** The component will calculate `cellWidth` and `dateRange` based on a selected mode.
- **Persistence:** Save the user's preferred zoom level to `localStorage` so it persists between sessions.
