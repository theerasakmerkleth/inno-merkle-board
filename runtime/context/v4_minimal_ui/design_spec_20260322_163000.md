## 1. UX/UI Vision: "Minimal Enterprise Grade"

Following the latest directive to elevate the platform to a "Minimal Design Enterprise Grade" standard, we are pivoting the visual language. The goal is to maximize data density while simultaneously reducing visual noise, cognitive load, and interface clutter. We will adopt a "Swiss Minimalist" approach tailored for heavy daily usage by enterprise professionals.

**Core Principles:**
- **Reduction of Borders & Shadows:** Remove heavy borders and drop shadows. Rely on whitespace, typography hierarchy, and subtle background color shifts (`bg-muted`) to delineate sections.
- **Sophisticated Monochromatic Palette:** Move away from high-contrast, aggressive dark modes to a refined, low-contrast "Zinc" or "Neutral" scale. Accent colors (Merkle Deep Red) will be used *exclusively* for primary actions and critical status indicators, not as decorative elements.
- **Typographic Discipline:** Strict use of sans-serif typefaces (e.g., Inter, Geist, or system-ui). Prioritize `text-sm` and `text-xs` with generous line heights (`leading-relaxed`) to handle dense information gracefully.
- **Ghost & Outline Components:** Replace solid secondary buttons with ghost or subtle outline variants.

## 2. Visual & Theming Guidelines (Tailwind CSS / shadcn)

### 2.1 Color Palette Update (The "Clean Slate" Theme)
*   **Backgrounds:** `bg-white` (Light Mode) or `bg-zinc-950` (Dark Mode).
*   **Surfaces (Cards/Modals):** `bg-zinc-50/50` (Light) or `bg-zinc-900/50` (Dark). No borders, or ultra-thin `border-zinc-100` / `border-zinc-800`.
*   **Text:** `text-zinc-900` (Primary), `text-zinc-500` (Secondary/Muted).
*   **Accents:** Merkle Deep Red (`bg-red-700`) is reserved *only* for the primary CTA on a page (e.g., "Create Task", "Sign In"). All other buttons are `variant="outline"` or `variant="ghost"`.

### 2.2 Component Re-Styling (shadcn/ui overrides)
*   **Cards (`<Card>`):** Remove `shadow-sm`. Use `shadow-none` and `border-zinc-200/50`.
*   **Badges (`<Badge>`):** Move from heavy pill shapes to minimal text-based tags or simple colored dot indicators next to plain text (e.g., a tiny red dot for "High Priority" instead of a solid red badge).
*   **Avatars (`<Avatar>`):** Smaller sizing (`h-8 w-8`), grayscale fallbacks.

## 3. Screen Redesign Specs

### 3.1 Authentication Flow (`/login`)
*   **Layout:** Centered, floating login form with zero borders and zero shadows. Just a clean typography hierarchy on a solid background.
*   **Inputs:** Underline-only inputs (`border-b` only, no full box) or very subtle filled inputs (`bg-zinc-100` in light mode) with no focus rings, just a bottom border highlight.

### 3.2 Kanban Board (`/`)
*   **Columns:** Remove the heavy background colors for columns. Columns should be transparent, separated only by generous horizontal padding (`gap-6`) and a subtle, muted column header.
*   **Task Cards:** 
    *   Flat design.
    *   Priority is indicated by a minimalist colored left-border (`border-l-2`) or a tiny status dot, rather than a full badge.
    *   Remove the Material Icon for AI unless absolutely necessary; if needed, make it a muted gray icon (`text-zinc-400`).
*   **Restricted Columns (RBAC):** Instead of a heavy red background (`bg-red-950/10`), simply reduce the opacity of the column (`opacity-50`) and show a sleek, minimalist `lock` icon in the header.

### 3.3 User Management (`/users`)
*   **Data Table:** Edge-to-edge design. Remove outer borders. Use ultra-thin row dividers. 
*   **Actions:** Replace standard dropdowns with minimalist "three-dot" context menus (`<DropdownMenu>`) to hide complex actions (Edit Role, Revoke Access) and keep the table view exceptionally clean.
