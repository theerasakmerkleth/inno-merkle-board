## 1. UX/UI Vision Alignment (Merkle Brand Guidelines 2025)

Moving away from the generic "Swiss Minimalist" dark mode, the platform must now strictly adhere to the **Merkle Brand Guidelines 2025 (v1.0)**. The design tokens have been updated to reflect the corporate identity, ensuring a unified experience for internal teams and clients.

### 1.1 Core Design Tokens (Extracted from Guidelines)
*   **Typography:** The primary font family is set to the official brand typeface (e.g., standardizing on Inter/Open Sans or custom webfonts specified in the PDF) for all Headings and Body text.
*   **Color Palette:**
    *   **Primary Brand Color:** Merkle's signature Red/Coral (e.g., `bg-merkle-red` mapping to the exact HEX from the PDF) used for primary action buttons (`<Button variant="default">`), active states, and focus rings.
    *   **Backgrounds:** Transitioning from harsh `zinc-950` to a refined, accessible Enterprise Light Mode by default, or a closely matched Dark Mode utilizing deep charcoal (`#1A1A1A`) instead of pure black.
    *   **Accents:** Utilizing secondary brand colors (Deep Navy/Blue) for sidebar navigation backgrounds and neutral grays for borders (`zinc-200` for light, `zinc-800` for dark).
*   **Logo & Assets:** The official Merkle logo (`project/inputs/merkle-website.png` or vector equivalent) MUST be placed prominently in the top-left of the Global Navigation Sidebar.

## 2. Structural Redesign (Wireframes & UI Components)

### 2.1 Global Navigation (Left Sidebar)
*   **Top Area (Brand & Workspace):**
    *   **Logo:** Merkle logo integrated at the top with a defined max-height (e.g., `h-8`) and appropriate padding.
    *   **Workspace Links:** `My Tasks` (Inbox), `Analytics Dashboard`, and `Directory` using custom Material Icons. Hover states use a subtle tint of the primary brand color.
*   **Middle Area (Projects):**
    *   The `Project Switcher` dropdown is restyled with brand-compliant typography.
    *   Active project links use a bold left-border (`border-l-4 border-merkle-red`) to indicate selection, rather than just a background change.

### 2.2 My Tasks View (`/my-tasks` or `/`)
*   **Layout:** A crisp, high-density data table (`shadcn/DataTable`).
*   **Styling:** Table headers (`<th>`) use uppercase, small tracking, and brand secondary colors. Status badges (`<Badge>`) use semantic brand colors (e.g., Green for Done, Yellow for In Progress) verified for WCAG AA contrast against the background.
*   **Interaction:** Row hover effects use a very light brand tint (`bg-merkle-red/5`).

### 2.3 Enterprise Analytics Dashboard (`/analytics`)
*   **Layout:** Grid of `shadcn/Card` components. Cards have a subtle shadow (`shadow-sm`) and soft border radius (`rounded-lg`) matching the 2025 guidelines.
*   **Data Visualization:**
    *   **Resource Loading Heatmap:** The progress bars must utilize the primary and secondary brand colors instead of default Indigo.
    *   **Cycle Time:** Large, legible typography for key metrics.

### 2.4 Task Modal Updates (Comments)
*   **Layout:** The `TaskModal` (`<Sheet>` or Dialog) adopts a cleaner, white/off-white background in Light Mode, or deep charcoal in Dark Mode.
*   **Comment Display:** 
    *   Avatar backgrounds use brand neutral colors.
    *   AI Agent comments are distinguished by a subtle brand-secondary (e.g., light blue/navy tint) background rather than generic indigo, aligning with the corporate AI persona guidelines.
    *   Primary action buttons ("Save Task", "Post") use the Merkle Primary Red.
